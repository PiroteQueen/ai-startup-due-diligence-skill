# Red-Team Checks

Run all nine contradiction checks before finalizing any recommendation. Each check pits the pitch narrative against the evidence ledger; record the verdict and supporting/contradicting evidence.

## Nine contradiction checks

1. **Pain vs willingness to pay**: Is there budget and urgency? A loud pain point without a budget line is a hobby, not a market.
2. **Demo vs workflow**: Does the product enter real operations, or just impress in a demo? Look for daily/weekly usage, integrations, and switching costs.
3. **AI wrapper vs compounding system**: Is there a durable data/workflow/distribution advantage, or only prompt engineering on top of a commodity model?
4. **Market size vs entry wedge**: Is there a narrow beachhead with credible expansion? Large TAM with no wedge is as bad as a wedge with no expansion path.
5. **Team story vs execution evidence**: Do past actions prove the claimed capability? Distinguish credentials from demonstrated resourcefulness.
6. **VC-fit vs good business**: Is this venture-scale, or a good non-VC business being forced into a VC narrative?
7. **Plan vs market evolution**: Is the initial business plan treated as a fixed promise even though the real job is finding product-market fit?
8. **Funding amount vs stage**: Does the raise buy the next evidence gate, or does it create survival risk / cultural corrosion / liquidation-preference overhang?
9. **Platform/runtime dependency vs moat**: Does the AI/cloud/platform layer transfer costs and risk to the company, the customer, or the platform; and can the company defend value if the platform changes?

## Risk onion layers

Peel risks layer by layer; each layer must have evidence, remaining risk, and a verification path:

- founder/team
- market
- competition
- timing
- financing
- marketing/CAC
- distribution/partner dependency
- technology
- product execution
- hiring/org
- location/ecosystem
- AI-specific dependency/compliance

## Post-raise operating risks

After a successful raise, check for:

- Hiring too many people before PMF or before the operating model is clear.
- Too many salespeople selling an unfinished / non-PMF product.
- Product and customer focus drifting after a large raise.
- Management culture becoming headcount-driven rather than output-driven.
- Urgency and deadline discipline decaying because runway feels abundant.
- Liquidation preference reducing exit optionality for founders and employees.
- Over-reliance on investor help that has not been partner-reference-checked.

## Investor-no diagnostics

If qualified investors passed, do not dismiss it as "they didn't get it". Diagnose which risk layer the no is really pointing at, then either produce counter-evidence or revise the plan. One no may be noise; repeated no's are data.
